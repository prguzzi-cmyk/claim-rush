A line-by-line review of what your insurance settlement should have included.

Most homeowners don't know what a settlement document should contain. Settlement IQ reads yours line by line and identifies what's missing, what's underpriced, how depreciation was applied, and where policy provisions may have been misread. The same review a public adjuster or attorney performs, applied to your specific document, your specific carrier, and your specific state.

What the report will tell you

  • Scope items the carrier may have omitted from your settlement
  • Unit pricing compared against regional norms for your county
  • Depreciation analysis — what was depreciated, whether it should have been, whether the schedule was applied correctly
  • Policy-provision audit — exclusions cited, limits applied, endorsements missed
  • Estimated recovery range and the statute-of-limitations window remaining

How it works

  1. Upload your document. PDF or DOCX of a settlement letter, estimate, or carrier statement.
  2. Forensic analysis runs. A multi-step review reads the document and checks it against state statutes, code requirements, regional pricing, and known carrier patterns. Typically under 90 seconds.
  3. Read your report. Delivered on screen and by email. Includes citations for every finding.
